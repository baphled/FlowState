# Pitch Evaluation Skill

## Purpose

This skill defines the 7-dimension scoring rubric for evaluating a startup pitch. Every analyst in the board-room swarm applies this rubric to their domain. The rubric provides consistent language for green flags, red flags, and decision weights.

---

## Dimension 1: Team

**What to evaluate:** Founder-market fit (has the team lived this problem?), relevant prior experience, complementary skills across the founding team, and demonstrated ability to recruit.

**Green flags:**
- Founders with direct experience of the pain point they are solving (operators, not observers)
- Prior exits or high-growth company experience in the same sector
- Complementary skills: technical + commercial, not three engineers or three salespeople
- Early team hires above the founders' own level in key functions

**Red flags:**
- No relevant domain experience — "we saw the opportunity in a report"
- All-technical or all-commercial founding team for a product requiring both
- Unusually high early attrition in the team (if disclosed)
- No clear culture of hiring well

**Weight:** High — most investors say team accounts for 40–50% of seed-stage decision

---

## Dimension 2: Market

**What to evaluate:** TAM credibility and methodology, growth rate and trajectory, timing (why now), and distribution risk.

**Green flags:**
- Bottom-up TAM with named customer segments and realistic pricing
- Market growing >20% YoY with structural tailwinds (regulation, technology shift, demographic change)
- Clear "why now" — a recent change has created a window that did not exist 3 years ago
- Distribution via existing high-intent channels (partner networks, regulatory mandates, community)

**Red flags:**
- Top-down TAM ("the market is $X billion, we only need 1%") — this is not a TAM analysis
- Shrinking or stagnant market with no structural catalyst
- "Viral growth" as the primary distribution strategy with no evidence
- Multiple competitive pivots already made

**Weight:** High — market size and timing determine the ceiling; a great team in a bad market still fails

---

## Dimension 3: Product

**What to evaluate:** Differentiation (what makes this product genuinely different?), defensibility (how hard is it to copy?), customer validation (who is paying and what are they saying?), and UX quality.

**Green flags:**
- Paying customers with documented, unprompted enthusiasm (NPS >50, qualitative quotes)
- Technical differentiation that takes months or years to replicate
- Network effects, proprietary data, or workflow lock-in
- Product built around the hardest part of the problem, not the easiest visible layer

**Red flags:**
- Demo product with no paying customers
- Differentiation is purely UX/design — easily copied
- Customer validation from family, friends, or warm intros only
- No answer to "why can't Salesforce/Microsoft/Google do this in 12 months?"

**Weight:** Medium — validated at seed; higher weight at Series A+

---

## Dimension 4: Traction

**What to evaluate:** Revenue (ARR/MRR), growth rate (MoM %), retention (logo churn, net revenue retention), NPS, and notable customers.

**Green flags:**
- MoM revenue growth >15% at early stage
- Net revenue retention >110% (customers expanding)
- At least one enterprise or notable brand-name customer
- Organic customer acquisition (inbound, referral, community)

**Red flags:**
- All revenue from one customer (concentration risk)
- Declining growth rate without explanation
- High logo churn (>20% annually) suggesting product-market fit problem
- Revenue from consulting/services masking weak SaaS metrics

**Weight:** Very high at Series A+; indicative at seed; the most honest signal available

---

## Dimension 5: Business Model

**What to evaluate:** Unit economics (LTV:CAC), path to profitability, scalability of the model, and gross margin.

**Green flags:**
- LTV:CAC > 3:1 with payback < 18 months
- Gross margin > 60% (SaaS) or clearly improving trajectory
- Clear, credible path to cash-flow positive within 24–36 months of this round
- Business model does not require linear headcount growth to scale revenue

**Red flags:**
- LTV:CAC < 1:1 — the company loses money on every customer acquired
- Gross margin < 40% with no clear path to improvement
- Revenue growth requiring proportional cost growth (services model masquerading as SaaS)
- "We'll figure out monetisation once we have scale" — red flag at any stage beyond pre-seed

**Weight:** High — unit economics determine whether venture scale returns are achievable

---

## Dimension 6: Defensibility

**What to evaluate:** Moat (what prevents a well-funded competitor from copying this in 12 months?), switching costs, network effects, IP or regulatory barriers.

**Green flags:**
- Proprietary data that compounds with usage (data flywheel)
- Network effects where value increases with each new user or customer
- Regulatory or compliance complexity that creates a moat by default
- High switching costs embedded in customer workflows

**Red flags:**
- "Our moat is our team" — people leave; this is not a moat
- Purely feature-based differentiation with no underlying structural advantage
- API-dependent business where the platform can replicate the use case
- No IP, no proprietary data, no network effects described

**Weight:** Medium at seed; increasingly important as capital raised grows

---

## Dimension 7: Capital Efficiency

**What to evaluate:** Runway (months at current burn), burn rate (absolute and burn multiple), use of funds clarity, and dilution to date.

**Green flags:**
- > 18 months runway post-close
- Burn multiple < 1.5 (for every £1 of net new ARR, burning < £1.50)
- Clear, specific use of funds tied to milestones that de-risk the next round
- Founder dilution < 30% at seed stage

**Red flags:**
- Runway < 12 months post-close with no clear path to next fundraise
- Burn multiple > 3 — burning capital faster than generating revenue
- Vague use of funds ("growth and product")
- Founder dilution > 50% — misaligned incentives for a long journey

**Weight:** High — capital efficiency predicts whether the company will survive to prove the thesis

---

## Summary Scoring

When using this rubric, score each dimension 1–5 and note the primary green or red flags observed. A pitch with three dimensions scoring < 2 is a `pass` regardless of other dimensions. A pitch with five dimensions scoring >= 4 is a strong `invest` candidate.
