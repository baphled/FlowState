# Skill: Statutory Language

## What I do

I provide the exact statutory phrasing requirements for Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs) and Annual Review outcome letters under the Children and Families Act 2014 and the SEND Code of Practice 2014. I distinguish enforceable provision language from aspirational language that would be challenged at tribunal.

## 1. Aspirational vs Legally Enforceable Provision Language

An EHCP provision is **legally enforceable** when it is:
- Specific enough for a parent or tribunal to verify compliance
- Quantified in terms of frequency, duration, and type of support
- Attributable to a named role or service (not "staff" in general)

An EHCP provision is **aspirational and unenforceable** when it uses:
- Conditional language: "where possible", "as appropriate", "when required"
- Access framing: "access to", "will have the opportunity to"
- Vague frequency: "regular", "frequent", "as needed"
- Generic staff references: "staff will support", "school will provide"

**Key principle from SEND CoP §9.69:** Section F provisions must be so specific and detailed that the local authority could be held to account for delivering them exactly as written.

## 2. Required Specificity in Section F — Educational Provision

Every entry in Section F must state ALL of the following:
1. **Type of support**: What is being provided (e.g., "1:1 speech and language therapy", "small-group literacy intervention")
2. **Frequency**: How often (e.g., "three times per week", "daily")
3. **Duration**: How long each session lasts (e.g., "30 minutes per session")
4. **Deliverer**: Who provides it (e.g., "a qualified Speech and Language Therapist", "a Teaching Assistant with Level 3 SEN qualification")
5. **Setting**: Where it is delivered (e.g., "in a withdrawal setting away from the classroom")

**Compliant Section F example:**
> "Speech and language therapy: 1:1 sessions of 45 minutes, delivered twice weekly by a qualified Speech and Language Therapist registered with the HCPC, in a dedicated therapy room within school. In addition, a classroom-based programme of 15 minutes daily, delivered by a Teaching Assistant trained by the SaLT to carry out the programme."

**Non-compliant Section F example (do not use):**
> "The school will provide access to speech and language support as required to meet the pupil's communication needs."

## 3. Prohibited Phrases

The following phrases MUST NOT appear in Sections B, C, D, E, F, G, or H:

| Prohibited phrase | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "access to" | Does not guarantee delivery |
| "as appropriate" | Conditional — unenforceable |
| "when possible" | Conditional — unenforceable |
| "as required" | Conditional — unenforceable |
| "staff will support" | No named role or qualification |
| "regular" | Frequency not quantified |
| "frequent" | Frequency not quantified |
| "where necessary" | Conditional — unenforceable |
| "support will be available" | Does not commit to delivery |
| "school-based support" | Deliverer not specified |

If any of these phrases appear in a draft, flag each instance with the section letter, quote the offending phrase, and provide a corrected alternative.

## 4. Formal Outcome Letter Structure

An Annual Review outcome letter must contain the following sections in order:

**Section 1 — Decision**
State clearly one of three outcomes:
- "The local authority has decided to maintain [child's name]'s EHCP without amendment."
- "The local authority has decided to amend [child's name]'s EHCP. A copy of the proposed amended plan is enclosed."
- "The local authority has decided to cease [child's name]'s EHCP. The reasons for this decision are set out below."

**Section 2 — Reasons**
Summarise the evidence considered: school view, family view, specialist input, progress against Section E outcomes. Reference specific outcomes by name. State whether each outcome has been met, partially met, or continues.

**Section 3 — Changes (if amending)**
List each section being amended and the nature of the change. For new provisions in Section F, provide the full compliant text.

**Section 4 — Family Rights**
Include verbatim:
> "If you disagree with this decision, you have the right to mediation before pursuing a tribunal appeal. Mediation must be considered before an appeal to the First-tier Tribunal (Special Educational Needs and Disability). You have two months from the date of this letter, or one month from the date of a mediation certificate (whichever is later), to appeal to the tribunal. For information about mediation and your rights, contact [LA SEND team contact details]."

**Section 5 — Next Steps and Timeline**
State the date of the next Annual Review (12 months from the current review meeting date). If the plan is being amended, state the date by which the final amended plan will be issued (within 8 weeks of the review meeting).

## 5. Consultation Window and Deadline Obligations

| Obligation | Statutory deadline |
|---|---|
| Outcome letter issued after meeting | Within 2 weeks of the Annual Review meeting |
| Family consultation on proposed amendments | 15 calendar days to respond |
| Final amended plan issued | Within 8 weeks of the Annual Review meeting |
| Next Annual Review scheduled | No later than 12 months from current review date |
| Formal meeting invitation sent | At least 2 weeks before the meeting date |

**Failure to meet these deadlines** constitutes a failure of statutory duty. If deadlines are at risk, flag immediately to the LA SEND manager and document the reason for delay in the case file.
