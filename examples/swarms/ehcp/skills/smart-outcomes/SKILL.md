# Skill: SMART Outcomes

## What I do

I ensure that every outcome written into Section E of an EHCP meets the SMART criteria as required by the SEND Code of Practice 2014 §9.66–9.69. I identify common failure patterns and provide worked rewrites.

## SMART Criteria for EHCP Section E

Each outcome in Section E must satisfy all five dimensions:

| Dimension | Definition for EHCP context |
|---|---|
| **S — Specific** | States exactly what the child or young person will do, achieve, or be able to do. Written from the child's perspective, not the adult's. |
| **M — Measurable** | Includes observable, verifiable criteria for success. A third party reading the outcome must be able to determine whether it has been met. |
| **A — Achievable** | Realistic for this specific child given their current level of need and ability as described in Sections B, C, and D. Aspirational but not impossible. |
| **R — Relevant** | Directly linked to an identified need in Section B (special educational needs), Section C (health needs), or Section D (social care needs). Does not introduce new needs not reflected in earlier sections. |
| **T — Time-bound** | States the review date by which the outcome will be assessed — typically 12 months from the Annual Review meeting date. |

## Three Worked Examples: Weak → Strong Rewrites

### Example 1 — Communication Need

**WEAK (fails M, fails S, fails T):**
> "Staff will support [child] to develop their communication skills."

**Problems:**
- Written for the adult ("staff will support"), not the child
- No measurable criteria — what does success look like?
- No time-bound review date
- "Communication skills" is not specific — which skills?

**STRONG (SMART-compliant):**
> "By [review date — 12 months], [child's name] will be able to independently use their AAC device to make requests, comment on activities, and answer simple questions in at least 3 out of 5 observed classroom interactions per week, as measured by weekly observation records completed by the class Teaching Assistant and reviewed termly by the Speech and Language Therapist."

---

### Example 2 — Literacy Need

**WEAK (fails M, fails A context, fails T):**
> "[Child] will make progress in reading."

**Problems:**
- "Progress" is unmeasurable without a baseline and target
- No time-bound date
- Not linked to a specific identified need or current level
- Could apply to any child — not specific to this child's situation

**STRONG (SMART-compliant):**
> "By [review date — 12 months], starting from a current standardised reading age of 6 years 4 months (assessed [date]), [child's name] will achieve a standardised reading age of at least 7 years 0 months as measured by the [named standardised assessment, e.g., York Assessment of Reading Comprehension], administered by the school's SEN team."

---

### Example 3 — Social and Emotional Need

**WEAK (fails S, fails M, fails R):**
> "[Child] will develop friendships and social skills."

**Problems:**
- "Develop friendships" is not measurable
- "Social skills" is vague — not linked to a specific need in Section B or D
- No criteria for what success looks like
- No review date

**STRONG (SMART-compliant):**
> "By [review date — 12 months], linked to the identified need in Section B for support with social communication and interaction (autism spectrum condition), [child's name] will independently initiate a conversation with a peer in an unstructured social setting (e.g., break time, lunch) on at least 3 occasions per week, as evidenced by weekly records completed by the ELSA/Learning Mentor and reviewed at each half-term pastoral meeting."

---

## Common Failure Patterns

### 1. Outcome written for the adult, not the child
**Signal phrases:** "Staff will...", "The school will...", "Support will be provided to..."
**Fix:** Rewrite starting with "[Child's name] will..." — the outcome describes what the child achieves, not what adults do.

### 2. No measurable criteria
**Signal phrases:** "will develop", "will improve", "will make progress", "will be supported to"
**Fix:** Add a specific, observable target with a named measurement tool or method.

### 3. No time-bound review date
**Signal phrases:** Any outcome without "By [date]..." or "Within 12 months..."
**Fix:** Add "By [Annual Review date, 12 months from today]..." at the start of every outcome.

### 4. Outcome copied unchanged from previous year
**Signal:** Identical wording to the previous plan's Section E with only the review date updated.
**Check:** Does the outcome reflect the child's current level and the updated evidence from the current Annual Review? If the child has met the previous outcome, a new, higher-level outcome is required. If they have not met it, the outcome may be retained but the provision supporting it must be reviewed.

### 5. Outcome not linked to a Section B/C/D need
**Fix:** Every outcome must begin with or include a reference to the specific identified need it addresses, e.g., "Linked to the identified need in Section B for support with..."

## Output Format for Section E

Each outcome entry should follow this template:

```
Outcome [N]: [Outcome title]
Linked need: Section [B/C/D] — [brief description of the identified need]
Baseline: [child's current level, with assessment date and tool where available]
Target: By [review date], [child's name] will [specific, measurable achievement].
Measurement: [How success will be measured, who will measure it, how often]
Progress at this review: [Met / Partially met / Not met — with evidence summary]
```
