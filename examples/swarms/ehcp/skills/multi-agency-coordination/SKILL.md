# Skill: Multi-Agency Coordination

## What I do

I provide the escalation paths, standard input types, and documentation requirements for gathering input from education, health, and social care for an EHCP Annual Review. I handle the practical reality that not all agencies respond on time — and I set out how to record an Annual Review as complete even with absent specialist input.

## Agencies and Expected Input Types

| Agency | Role | Expected input | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| School SENCO | Education lead | School view form: progress against outcomes, current needs, proposed changes, attendance | Completed form or letter |
| Educational Psychologist (EP) | Cognition & learning, ASC | Updated psychological advice; opinion on whether educational provision remains appropriate | Written report, typically 2–4 pages |
| CAMHS | Mental health, emotional regulation | Updated clinical letter or report; current diagnosis, medication, therapy regime, recommendations | Clinical letter |
| Paediatric health team | Physical health, developmental paediatrics | Updated health advice; any new diagnoses, medication changes, therapy recommendations | Clinical letter |
| Occupational Therapist (OT) | Sensory, motor, self-care | Therapy report; current targets, recommended provision frequency | Written report |
| Speech and Language Therapist (SaLT) | Communication, AAC | Updated SaLT report; current targets, recommended provision frequency and deliverer | Written report |
| Social Care (CSWT/CST) | Social care needs, family support | Updated social care advice; current support package; Child in Need or Child Protection plan status | Written advice or form |
| Attendance and Welfare Officer | School attendance | Attendance data for the academic year; any referrals or interventions | Data extract or letter |

## Escalation Path for Late or Missing Input

For each agency that has not submitted input by the request deadline, follow this escalation sequence:

**Stage 1 — Email chase (Day 0 after deadline)**
Send a polite chaser email to the named professional and their team inbox. State:
- The child's name, date of birth, and EHCP reference number
- The original request date
- The Annual Review meeting date
- The new submission deadline (typically 5 working days from the chase email)
- Contact details for the LA EHCP Coordinator

Document: date sent, recipient, method.

**Stage 2 — Phone chase (Day 5 after deadline if no response)**
Telephone the named professional directly. If unavailable, leave a voicemail and send a follow-up email. Document:
- Date and time of call
- Who was spoken to (or voicemail left)
- Any reason given for delay
- Revised commitment (if given)

**Stage 3 — Written escalation to service lead (Day 10 after deadline)**
Write formally to the professional's service lead (e.g., CAMHS Team Manager, OT Service Lead). State:
- Input has been requested twice with no response
- The Annual Review meeting date is approaching / has passed
- The failure to provide input will be noted in the outcome letter
- Request a response within 3 working days

Document: date sent, recipient, method.

**Stage 4 — Flag to SEND Manager (Meeting day or after — no response received)**
If no input has been received by the meeting date, flag the gap to the LA SEND Manager. Include:
- A log of all chase attempts (dates, methods, responses)
- The impact on the Annual Review outcome (e.g., "we cannot update Section G without OT input")
- A recommended course of action (proceed with review noting the gap; defer meeting; seek alternative evidence)

## How to Handle Missing CAMHS Input When on a Waiting List

This is the most common gap scenario. CAMHS waiting lists are frequently 12–18 months, meaning a child may have been referred but not yet seen.

**What to record in the case file:**
- Date of CAMHS referral
- Referral reason
- Confirmation from CAMHS (letter or email) that the child is on the waiting list
- Any interim support arrangements in place (e.g., school-based counselling, Early Help)

**What to write in the outcome letter:**
> "CAMHS input was requested for this Annual Review. [Child's name] is currently on the CAMHS waiting list following a referral on [date]. No updated clinical advice has been received. The current Section C health provisions relating to emotional wellbeing have been retained pending CAMHS assessment. This will be reviewed at the next Annual Review or sooner if CAMHS provides advice before then."

**What NOT to do:**
- Do not remove health provisions from Section C/G simply because CAMHS has not responded
- Do not substitute a school counsellor's notes for a clinical CAMHS report
- Do not mark the Annual Review as complete without noting the gap in the case file

## Recording an Annual Review as "Complete" With Absent Specialist Input

An Annual Review can be recorded as complete even if one or more specialist reports are absent, provided:

1. All chase stages above were followed and documented
2. The gap is explicitly noted in the outcome letter (see wording above)
3. The relevant EHCP sections (C/D/G/H) are retained as-is with a note that they are pending updated specialist input
4. Any open actions (e.g., "chase OT report for mid-year review") are recorded in the case file with an owner and target date
5. The SEND Manager has been informed if the gap is significant (e.g., missing EP advice when an amendment to Section B is under consideration)

**Marking the case:** Record as "Annual Review Complete — [date] — Note: [agency] input outstanding. See case file actions."

## Standard Input Request Template

Use this template when first requesting input from any specialist:

---
Subject: Annual Review input request — [Child's name], DOB [DD/MM/YYYY], EHCP Ref [XXX]

Dear [Professional's name],

I am writing to request your contribution to the Annual Review of [child's name]'s Education, Health and Care Plan.

The Annual Review meeting is scheduled for [date]. Could you please provide updated advice on [child's name]'s current [health/social care/specialist] needs and any recommendations for the coming year's provision?

Please return your report or written advice to [coordinator email] by [deadline — 2 weeks before meeting].

If you are unable to provide input by this date, please let me know as soon as possible so I can update the case file accordingly.

[Coordinator name, title, contact details]

---

## Documentation Standards

All chase attempts must be recorded in the case management system with:
- Date
- Method (email/phone/letter)
- Recipient name and role
- Outcome (sent, spoken to, voicemail, no response, input received)

This log is the LA's evidence of procedural compliance if a family or tribunal later challenges the completeness of the Annual Review.
