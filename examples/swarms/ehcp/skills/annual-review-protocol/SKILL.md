# Skill: Annual Review Protocol

## What I do

I provide the complete 10-step EHCP Annual Review workflow with all statutory timing obligations. I am the authoritative reference for what must happen, in what order, by when, and what constitutes a statutory failure.

## The 10-Step Annual Review Workflow

### Step 1 — Identify Cases Due Within the Next 4 Weeks

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**When:** Rolling — check the case management system weekly
**Action:**
- Pull all EHCPs whose review date falls within the next 28 days
- For each case, confirm the child's current school placement is correct in the system
- Flag any cases where the child has changed school since the last review — these require updated school details before invitations can be issued
- Flag any cases approaching Year 9 (age 13–14) — these require a transition review focus under SEND CoP §9.131–9.135, with involvement from post-16 providers and careers guidance

**Output:** List of cases to be reviewed, with school placement confirmed.

---

### Step 2 — Issue Formal Meeting Invitation

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Statutory deadline:** At least 2 weeks before the meeting date
**Mandatory attendees:**
- Parent/carer (or young person aged 16+)
- School SENCO (or designated SEN contact)
- Any professional whose advice is being sought (Educational Psychologist, CAMHS, Health, Social Care — if they have current involvement)

**Action:**
- Send a formal written invitation by letter and email, stating the meeting date, time, venue (or video link), and purpose
- Enclose or link to the current EHCP for reference
- Include the school view form and family view form with a return deadline (2 weeks before the meeting, or by a specified date)
- For young people aged 16+, send the invitation directly to the young person as well as to any parent/carer

**Failure mode:** Sending the invitation fewer than 2 weeks before the meeting is a procedural failure. If a meeting must be rescheduled for this reason, document why and issue a corrected invitation.

---

### Step 3 — Request School View and Family View

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Return deadline:** At least 2 weeks before the meeting (i.e., at the same time as the invitation, with a response deadline set accordingly)
**Forms required:**
- School view: progress against each Section E outcome; updated description of current needs; any proposed changes to provision; attendance data; exclusion history; any new specialist involvement
- Family view: family's assessment of progress; child/young person's own views (Section A update); any concerns about the current plan; any changes in health or social care needs; views on the placement

**Chase protocol:** If forms are not returned by the deadline, phone the school SENCO and family individually. Document all chase attempts with date and outcome.

---

### Step 4 — Collate All Submissions

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Window:** 2 weeks before the meeting
**Action:**
- Compile school view, family view, and any specialist reports received
- For each Section E outcome, record: (a) school's assessment (Met/Partially met/Not met), (b) family's assessment, (c) any specialist evidence
- Flag any specialist reports that have not been received — see `multi-agency-coordination` skill for escalation paths
- Prepare a one-page summary for the meeting chair listing: current outcomes, progress indicators, proposed changes flagged by any party, and any disputes between school and family views

**Failure mode:** Proceeding to a review meeting without a school view is procedurally weak. Proceeding without a family view (where the family has not disengaged) is more serious — document every attempt to obtain it.

---

### Step 5 — Hold the Annual Review Meeting

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator (chair), SENCO, family/young person, relevant specialists
**Mandatory agenda items:**
1. Welcome and introductions — confirm who is present and their role
2. Purpose: review of EHCP against the child's current needs and progress
3. Progress against each Section E outcome — go through each outcome in turn
4. Update on the child's health and social care needs (Sections C and D)
5. Child/young person's views (Section A) — if the child is not present, how were their views gathered?
6. Family's views on progress, the current plan, and any proposed changes
7. School's views on progress and proposed amendments
8. Discussion of any proposed changes to the plan
9. Outcome decision: maintain / amend / cease
10. Next steps and timeline

**Minute-taking:** Minutes must be taken. A copy must be sent to all attendees within 2 weeks.

---

### Step 6 — Draft the Outcome Letter

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator / Drafter agent
**Deadline:** Same day or next working day after the meeting — the 2-week clock for issuing the letter starts from the meeting date
**Content:** See `statutory-language` skill, Section 4 (Formal Outcome Letter Structure)
**If amending:** Prepare the proposed amended EHCP alongside the outcome letter. The amended plan is sent to the family for a 15-day consultation period.

---

### Step 7 — 15-Day Consultation Period (If Amending)

**Who:** Family/young person
**Window:** 15 calendar days from receipt of the proposed amended plan
**Action by LA:**
- Send proposed amended plan and outcome letter together
- Note the deadline on the case file
- Chase at Day 10 if no response received
- If the family requests a meeting to discuss proposed amendments, this must be offered

**If no response by Day 15:** The LA may proceed to finalise the amended plan. Document that the consultation period expired without response.

---

### Step 8 — Issue Final Outcome Letter / Final Amended Plan

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Statutory deadline:** Within 2 weeks of the Annual Review meeting (for maintain/cease decisions); within 8 weeks of the meeting (for amended plans, after the 15-day consultation)
**Action:**
- For "maintain" decisions: issue the outcome letter within 2 weeks of the meeting
- For "amend" decisions: issue the final amended EHCP within 8 weeks of the meeting (the 15-day consultation runs within this 8-week window)
- For "cease" decisions: issue the outcome letter within 2 weeks; advise family of their right to appeal

**Failure mode:** Issuing the final amended plan after the 8-week deadline is a statutory failure. If this is unavoidable, document the reason and notify the SEND manager.

---

### Step 9 — Notify School of Changes

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Deadline:** Within 5 working days of issuing the final amended plan
**Action:**
- Send a copy of the final amended EHCP to the school
- If the placement (Section I) has changed, notify the new school and confirm transfer arrangements
- If Section F provisions have changed, confirm with the school how the new provisions will be resourced and when they will start

---

### Step 10 — Close the Cycle and Set Next Review Date

**Who:** LA EHCP Coordinator
**Action:**
- Record the completion date on the case management system
- Set the next Annual Review trigger for 12 months from the current review meeting date (not the date the letter was issued)
- File all correspondence, forms, and meeting minutes in the case file
- If there are outstanding actions (e.g., awaiting a specialist report that did not arrive in time), record these as open actions with an owner and target date

**Output:** Case marked "Annual Review Complete — [date]". Next review date set. Case file updated.

## Timing Summary Table

| Obligation | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Invitation to all attendees | At least 2 weeks before meeting |
| School view and family view forms returned | At least 2 weeks before meeting |
| Outcome letter issued | Within 2 weeks of meeting |
| Consultation period (if amending) | 15 calendar days |
| Final amended plan issued | Within 8 weeks of meeting |
| Minutes sent to all attendees | Within 2 weeks of meeting |
| Next review date set | 12 months from current meeting date |
