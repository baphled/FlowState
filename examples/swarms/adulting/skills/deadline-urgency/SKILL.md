# Skill: Deadline Urgency

## What I do

I calibrate the tone and escalation level of formal correspondence based on deadline proximity and the statutory or contractual context of the dispute or request. Three tiers — Standard, Urgent, Escalation — map to concrete language templates.

## Tone Tiers

### Tier 1 — Standard (14+ days remaining or no stated deadline)

Use when: first contact, proactive enquiry, or response well within deadline.

Characteristics:
- Polite, cooperative register
- Assumes good faith on both sides
- States the purpose clearly without pressure language
- Ends with a specific but non-threatening follow-up request ("Please confirm receipt by [date]")

Opening pattern: "I am writing to [enquire about / provide information regarding / request clarification on] …"

### Tier 2 — Urgent (7–14 days remaining, or second contact after no response)

Use when: deadline is imminent, a previous letter was unanswered, or an automated payment has failed.

Characteristics:
- Firm but professional tone
- Explicitly states the deadline and consequence of inaction
- References any previous correspondence by date
- Requests a response within a specific shorter window ("I request a response by [date], which allows [N] working days")
- Does not threaten legal action yet but signals escalation is possible

Opening pattern: "I refer to my previous correspondence dated [date] / I am writing as a matter of urgency regarding …"

### Tier 3 — Escalation (fewer than 7 days remaining, overdue, or formal complaint)

Use when: deadline has passed, previous contacts were ignored, an error by the organisation has caused financial harm, or a statutory right is being infringed.

Characteristics:
- Formal complaint register
- Cites statutory basis where applicable (see legislation table below)
- Provides a numbered chronology of prior contact
- States the specific remedy requested
- Sets a short hard deadline before further escalation ("I require resolution by [date], failing which I will escalate to [regulator / ombudsman / small claims]")
- Signs off formally ("Yours faithfully" for unknown recipient, "Yours sincerely" for named)

Opening pattern: "I write to make a formal complaint regarding … / I write in accordance with [Act/regulation] …"

## Legislation and Regulator Reference

| Organisation type | Relevant legislation / regulator | Escalation route |
|---|---|---|
| HMRC | Taxes Management Act 1970; Finance Acts | HMRC Complaints → Adjudicator's Office → Parliamentary Ombudsman |
| Council tax | Local Government Finance Act 1992 | Council complaints procedure → Local Government Ombudsman |
| Utility (energy) | Energy Act 2023; Ofgem licence conditions | Supplier complaints → Energy Ombudsman (after 8 weeks) |
| Utility (water) | Water Industry Act 1991 | Supplier complaints → Consumer Council for Water (CCW) |
| Financial institution | Consumer Credit Act 1974; FCA CONC rules | FCA-regulated complaints → Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) |
| Landlord | Landlord and Tenant Act 1985; Housing Act 2004 | Council Environmental Health → Housing Ombudsman |
| NHS | NHS Constitution 2015 | PALS → Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman |

## Letter Templates

### Standard enquiry / first contact
```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Recipient Name or "The Complaints Team"]
[Organisation]
[Address]

Dear [Sir/Madam / Name],

Re: [Account / Reference / Case number if known]

I am writing to [state purpose — e.g., "request clarification on the council tax account referenced above"].

[Body: 1–2 short paragraphs with specific facts, dates, and amounts. No padding.]

I would be grateful if you could confirm receipt of this letter and provide a response within [14 / 21] days.

Yours faithfully / sincerely,
[Signature]
[Name]
[Contact number / email]
```

### Urgent second contact
```
[Date]

Re: [Account / Reference] — URGENT: Response Required by [Date]

I refer to my letter of [previous date], to which I have not yet received a response.

I am writing as a matter of urgency. The deadline for [action] is [date], which is [N] days from today. Failure to resolve this by that date will result in [specific consequence — e.g., missed payment deadline, accruing interest].

I request a written response by [date — typically 5–7 working days from today].

[If previous contact by phone, include: "I also telephoned on [date] and spoke with [name / 'an adviser'] who [outcome]."]

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
```

### Escalation / formal complaint
```
[Date]

FORMAL COMPLAINT — Re: [Account / Reference]

I write to make a formal complaint regarding [organisation]'s handling of [specific issue].

Chronology of contact:
1. [Date]: [Action taken — e.g., "Submitted payment via online portal; confirmation ref. XYZ"]
2. [Date]: [Follow-up — e.g., "Received demand notice despite payment"]
3. [Date]: [Further contact]

The remedy I require is:
- [Specific action — e.g., "Immediate acknowledgement that payment of £X was received on [date]"]
- [If financial harm: "Reimbursement of [charges / interest] totalling £X"]
- [Written confirmation within [N] working days]

If this matter is not resolved by [specific date], I will escalate to [regulator / ombudsman] and, where appropriate, pursue recovery through the small claims court.

I enclose / attach: [list supporting documents — statements, screenshots, previous letters].

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
```

## Worked Example

**Situation**: Council tax payment was made on 1 April but a liability order hearing is scheduled for 15 April. Eleven days remain.

**Tier**: 2 — Urgent (11 days, previous notice ignored / not acted on)

**Key elements to include**:
- Reference the payment date and method (bank transfer / direct debit)
- Include the transaction reference
- Request the hearing be vacated as payment has been made
- Cite Local Government Finance Act 1992, s.33 (liability orders require non-payment; payment extinguishes the basis)
- Request written confirmation by 10 April (5 days before hearing)
