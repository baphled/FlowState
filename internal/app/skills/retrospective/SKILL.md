# Retrospective Skill

Learn from every significant task. After completing work, ask: What went well? What went wrong? What would I do differently? What pattern is worth capturing for reuse?

Retrospectives prevent repeating mistakes and accelerate learning. They transform individual experiences into institutional knowledge. Apply this after task completion, after failures, and when discovering patterns that might help others.

Document findings in memory-keeper for future reference. Share learnings with the team. Build a culture of continuous improvement where every task makes the next one easier.
